Methods of Rainwater Harvesting

Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting

Rooftop rainwater harvesting is one of the most common methods of collecting rainwater. Rainwater is collected from the roof of buildings and stored in tanks or directed to recharge groundwater levels. This method is highly effective for residential and commercial buildings, especially in urban areas.

Surface Runoff Harvesting

Surface runoff harvesting involves collecting water that flows over the ground surface, often directed to ponds, tanks, or reservoirs. This method is especially useful in areas where there is heavy rainfall or during monsoon seasons to prevent water wastage.

In-Ground Rainwater Harvesting Pits

This method uses pits or trenches to collect rainwater, which then slowly percolates into the ground, recharging groundwater levels. It is highly effective in rural areas or for larger facilities where there is space to dig pits.

Percolation Tanks

Percolation tanks are artificially created bodies of water that capture and store rainwater, allowing it to percolate through the soil to recharge groundwater. This method is commonly used in regions with low groundwater levels.
